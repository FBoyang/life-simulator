Digital Fly

Tiny Connectome Simulator

Sensory neurons sample the arena, spikes flow through a small connectome, and motor neurons shape the virtual fly's motion.

Silence a neuron to test its causal role in sugar seeking, bitter avoidance, and forward movement.
Drag sugar in the arena. Use left and right arrows to steer; release them to return to autopilot.

Model idea

Sensory neurons receive environmental input; the tiny connectome propagates spikes; motor neurons control movement; neuron silencing tests causal roles.

By default the fly tends to approach sugar, avoids bitter when it is active, and uses left/right light sensors to steer toward the lamp.
